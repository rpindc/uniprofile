UniProfile guide

How it works

A plain-language guide — what UniProfile is, what it does, and what it honestly doesn't.

01

What UniProfile is

Most travelers are scattered data. Your passport number lives in one airline system, your KTN in another, your meal preference somewhere only one hotel remembered, your loyalty number re-typed at every checkout. UniProfile doesn't replace any of that — it gives you one place to hold your own travel identity, so you're never starting from zero. Add your passport once. Set your seat preference once. When you join a group trip, your readiness is already there. Your data stays yours: you control what you share and with whom, and you can delete it at any time.

Getting started — in five steps

1. Set up your profile. Add your legal name, date of birth, and nationality once. This is the foundation everything else builds on, and you'll never have to re-enter it.

2. Add your documents to DocVault. Store your passport, visas, and any travel documents in one secure place. UniProfile keeps track of their expiry dates so you don't have to.

3. Add a trip — or just forward your booking. Create a trip manually, or forward your confirmation email to trips@uniprofile.net and UniProfile will read it and build the trip for you — no typing needed.

4. Check your readiness with DocCheck. For any trip, DocCheck tells you whether your documents will clear your destination's entry requirements — before you travel, not at the airport.

5. Keep an eye on your dashboard. UniProfile watches your trips and documents and surfaces alerts — an approaching passport expiry, an entry-rule issue — right on your home dashboard, so nothing catches you out.

For group trips, create a Journey to see everyone's readiness in one place — no more chasing seven separate email threads.

02

You own your travel identity

Every airline, hotel, and booking platform holds a fragment of your travel life. They share it within their own systems — they don't share it with each other, or with you. UniProfile works the other way: it's your record, not a vendor's. You maintain your documents, preferences, and emergency contacts in one place. You decide what goes out and to whom.

03

DocVault — your documents, held by you

Add your passport, visas or residency permits, trusted traveler numbers (KTN, Global Entry), driver's license, and travel insurance here. UniProfile tracks expiry dates and surfaces alerts when something needs attention.

One important clarification: we store what you enter — we do not verify documents with any government database. You still need the physical document at the border. Sensitive numbers are stored masked; you'll see the last four digits on screen, not the full string.

04

Journeys — your trips as one record

A Journey is your trip as one unified record. Travel solo and it's your own complete picture — itinerary, documents, and readiness in one place. Travel with others and everyone shares that same view: who has documents on file, whose passport is expiring, who still needs to be added. Think of it as one version of the trip — not seven separate email threads.

05

DocCheck — what "on file" means

When UniProfile says a document is "on file," it means you've entered it — not that an algorithm has cleared you for travel. Entry decisions are made by people at borders. A UniProfile reviewer checks entry requirements against current sources for your travel details, but we can't guarantee they're current or complete for every situation.

"Passport on file — check entry rules for your destination" is what we'll say. "You're cleared to travel" is not something UniProfile would say. Travel insurance is shown as on file with coverage dates — not as proof of coverage.

06

Sharing & privacy — what you share, with whom

Today, sharing happens within Journeys. When you join as a linked member, you choose whether to approve the organizer's request to see your readiness status. If you approve, the organizer sees whether your documents are on file — not the document details themselves. If you don't, they see only that you're pending. Named travelers (children or guests the organizer manages) work differently — the organizer enters their documents directly on their behalf.

Coming soon

One day you'll be able to share just what's needed at the moment — your seat preference and meal code with a hotel at check-in, or your KTN to an airline without forwarding your passport. Per trip, per vendor, chosen by you. That capability is in development and not available yet.
